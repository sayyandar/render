Online Safety Tips

Protect yourself from phishing and online threats

1. Always Check URLs

  • Hover over links before clicking
  • Look for HTTPS in the address bar
  • Verify domain names carefully
  • Check for misspellings or odd characters

2. Email Safety

  • Never click links in unsolicited emails
  • Check sender email addresses carefully
  • Be wary of urgent or threatening language
  • Look for poor grammar or spelling errors

3. Password Security

  • Use unique passwords for each account
  • Enable two-factor authentication
  • Use a password manager
  • Never share passwords via email or text

4. Software Protection

  • Keep all software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Enable firewalls
  • Regularly back up important data

5. Personal Information

  • Never share personal info via email
  • Verify requests through official channels
  • Monitor financial statements regularly
  • Use credit monitoring services

6. Mobile Security

  • Only download apps from official stores
  • Check app permissions carefully
  • Use secure Wi-Fi networks
  • Enable remote wipe capabilities

If You Suspect Phishing

1

DO NOT click any links or download attachments

2

DO NOT provide any personal information

3

Report the incident to your IT department or service provider

4

Change passwords if you suspect compromise

5

Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity
